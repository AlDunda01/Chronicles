Fantasy

About the Genre

Fantasy, or Fantasy Fiction is a genre in which imagination and creativity reign supreme. Resulting in many different aspects of world-building that brings mythical creatures, magic, and epics to life. From ancient mythologies like Greek mythology, Norse mythology, and Mesopotamian epics to JRR Tolkien's Middle Earth, C.S. Lewis's Narinia, and George RR Martin's Westeros.

Authors

  • JRR Tolkien: Often known as "The Father of Modern Fantasy", John Ronald Reuel Tolkien crafted his masterpiece known as Middle Earth which not only allowed his works like The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings to explode in popularity, but has also inspired the modern archictect of Fantasy fiction.
  • George R.R. Martin: Inspired by Tolkien's Middle Earth, George Raymond Richard Martin took a sharp turn from his idol's work by instead crafting his world to be more morally grey and dark from Middle Earth. The following product, Westeros is the sprawling world that is the hub of his book series and TV show Game of Thrones.
  • Kentaro Miura: A Japanese Manga(Graphic Novel) Artist, Kentaro Miura took inspiration from both Western and Eastern sources. He created a world similar to Martin's and Tolkien's but also added twists from his native Japan. The result birthed the highly acclaimed Dark Fantasy Manga Berserk.
  • Hidetaka Miyazaki: Another Fantasy lover hailing from Japan, Miyazaki grew up and admired Miura's Berserk. But rather then heading down the literature path, Miyazaki took his genius to the Video Game department. He joined up with Japanese video game company Fromsoftware to create a series of RPG games that broke the gaming industry. Miyazaki's Soulsborne genre became very acclaimed for its challenging mechanics and overdose of fantasy setting.

Types

  • High/Epic Fantasy:These stories take place in huge, fully built worlds with detailed histories, politics, and magic systems. They often involve epic battles between good and evil, grand quests, and world-shaping stakes.
  • Low Fantasy:Low fantasy stories insert magical or fantastical elements into an otherwise real or gritty world. They tend to be morally gray and more focused on human politics, survival, and realistic consequences than epic heroism.
  • Dark Fantasy: Grimdark fantasy is gritty, morally ambiguous, and often violent, showing harsh, unforgiving worlds. Heroes are flawed or anti-heroes, and betrayal, tragedy, and death are common.
  • Swords and Sorcery:These stories focus on fast-paced adventures with individual heroes or small groups, often featuring personal quests, combat, and magic. Stakes are more personal than world-altering.

Worlds

  • Middle Earth: A vast and very richly detailed world of many wonders, Tolkien's main hub is a home for Elves, Dwarves, Goblins, Hobbits, and Humans. From the peaceful Shire to the volcanic lands of Mordor, Middle Earth has sprinkled a bit of everything that makes it so revolutionary.
  • Narnia:Narnia is a magical land created by C.S. Lewis, filled with talking animals, mythical creatures, and enchanted landscapes. From the snowy forests to the majestic castles, it’s a realm where heroes embark on epic quests, battles of good versus evil unfold, and ordinary children can discover extraordinary destinies.
  • Westeros:Westeros, crafted by George R.R. Martin, is a vast and treacherous land where noble houses vie for power, dragons soar through the skies, and winters can last for decades. Filled with political intrigue, moral ambiguity, and epic battles, it’s a world where alliances shift, heroes rise and fall, and the line between good and evil is never clear.
  • Ancient Greece:The world of Homer’s Odyssey is a sprawling, ancient landscape filled with gods, monsters, and perilous seas. From the treacherous island of the Cyclopes to the enchanting realm of the Sirens, it’s a place where cunning, bravery, and endurance are tested, and every journey is an epic adventure across myth and legend.
